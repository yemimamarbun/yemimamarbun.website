Introduction to Past Tenses

Past tenses in English are used to talk about actions or states that occurred in the past. There are four main past tenses in English:

  • Simple Past
  • Past Continuous
  • Past Perfect
  • Past Perfect Continuous

Each of these tenses has specific uses and forms that help convey exactly when an action took place and whether it was completed or ongoing.

Simple Past Tense

Form

For regular verbs: Base form + -ed (e.g., worked, played, watched)

For irregular verbs: Use the past form (e.g., went, ate, wrote)

Positive: She worked yesterday.

Negative: She did not work yesterday.

Question: Did she work yesterday?

Usage

  • Completed actions in the past
  • Past habits or states
  • Sequences of actions in the past

I visited Paris last summer.

When I was a child, I played football every day.

He woke up, had breakfast, and went to work.

Past Continuous Tense

Form

Was/Were + present participle (-ing form)

Positive: They were watching TV at 8 pm yesterday.

Negative: They were not watching TV at 8 pm yesterday.

Question: Were they watching TV at 8 pm yesterday?

Usage

  • Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
  • Interrupted actions in the past
  • Parallel actions in the past
  • Setting the scene in a story

At 6 pm yesterday, I was cooking dinner.

I was reading when the phone rang.

While I was studying, my brother was playing video games.

The sun was shining, and birds were singing.

Past Perfect Tense

Form

Had + past participle

Positive: She had finished her work before the meeting started.

Negative: She had not finished her work before the meeting started.

Question: Had she finished her work before the meeting started?

Usage

  • Actions completed before another action in the past
  • Experiences up to a certain point in the past
  • Unrealized hopes or wishes in the past

When we arrived at the cinema, the film had already started.

I had never seen such a beautiful sunset before that day.

I wish I had studied harder for the test.

Past Perfect Continuous Tense

Form

Had been + present participle (-ing form)

Positive: He had been working there for ten years before he retired.

Negative: He had not been working there for long before he quit.

Question: Had he been working there long before he quit?

Usage

  • Duration of an action up to a certain point in the past
  • Cause of something in the past
  • Actions that were recently stopped

She had been waiting for over an hour when the bus finally arrived.

His hands were dirty because he had been gardening.

I had been studying all day, so I was tired.
